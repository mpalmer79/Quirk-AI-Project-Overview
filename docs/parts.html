Quirk AI for Parts

Status: In Development

AI powered tools designed for parts advisors and our customers. Our goal is to modernize the transactional process for Retail, Wholesale and Internal workflow by maximizing the use of automated efficiency, LLM and machine learning — powered by Quirk AI.

Open Close Scale & Scope of QuirkParts

Operation Overview

  • Multi-brand OEM parts operation serving retail, wholesale, and internal service.
  • Centralized high-capacity warehouse footprint with rapid fulfillment.
  • Brand-specific storefronts (e.g., Dodge, Subaru, Jeep) to drive fitment accuracy and conversion.

Core Strengths

  • Deep catalog coverage across major OEMs.
  • National e-commerce reach plus regional B2B delivery routes.
  • Service lane integration (VIN-first lookups, technician parts carts).

Key Opportunities

  • Network-wide demand forecasting to tighten turns and reduce aged stock.
  • Robotics/ASRS for faster picks in small-parts, fast-mover zones.
  • Unified data layer for pricing, availability, and fitment across channels.

Open Close Advanced Tech for Parts Operations (2025)

AI / LLM Use-Cases

  • Natural-language & VIN search: “Clip for trunk latch on 2018 Wrangler” → exact OEM PN with supersessions.
  • Image recognition: Photo → part identification, catalog diagram hot-spotting.
  • Advisor copilot: Cross-catalog lookup, price/availability, warranty & TSB references in one console.
  • Conversational CX: Web chat & phone IVR that quotes stock/price, creates carts, and schedules pickup/ship.

Machine Learning

  • Demand forecasting: seasonal & VIN-park trends; hub vs spoke stocking; automatic reorder points.
  • Dynamic pricing: elasticity by channel (retail, wholesale, marketplace), competitive crawls, margin guardrails.
  • Slotting optimization: ABC velocity → shelf/VLM locations to minimize walk time.

Automation & Robotics

  • ASRS / VLMs: Goods-to-person for small SKUs; integrates with pick-to-light and DMS.
  • AMRs & conveyors: Zone pickups & tote routing; batch-picking waves for high-volume times.
  • Cycle-count automation: Vision robots/drones for nightly counts; 99%+ location accuracy.

IoT & Data Fabric

  • RFID/weight-sensing bins for auto-decrement; mislabeled/empty-bin alerts.
  • Unified event stream (orders, picks, receipts) → real-time dashboards & anomaly detection.
  • APIs to DMS/ERP/e-comm: Reynolds/CDK, PartsLink, Shopify/Headless, WMS.

Open Close Action Plan for Quirk AI — Parts

Phase 0 — Foundations (30–60 days)

  • Stand up a unified parts index (catalog + supersessions + price + on-hand + lead-time).
  • Expose a secure Parts Graph API (fitment, substitutes, availability) to web/app/advisors.
  • Instrument events: search → add-to-cart → pick → ship; build live dashboards.

Phase 1 — Advisor & Customer Intelligence (60–120 days)

  • Deploy LLM Advisor Copilot (VIN decode, multi-catalog lookup, price/ETA, upsell kits).
  • Add image-to-part beta on site + internal tool for counter team.
  • Launch conversational chat/IVR for stock, quotes, order status, and B2B reorders.

Phase 2 — Inventory Science (120–180 days)

  • ML forecasting per SKU/site; auto min/max & reorder proposals with human approval.
  • Slotting optimizer: re-map fast movers near pack stations; measure walk-time saved.
  • Dynamic pricing with guardrails by channel and customer segment.

Phase 3 — Automation (pilot → scale)

  • Pilot ASRS/VLM for small parts; integrate pick-to-light & WMS.
  • Introduce AMRs for tote moves; wave picking for batch orders.
  • Nightly vision cycle counts; exception queue to human.

KPIs to Track

  • Fill rate, backorder rate, days on hand, inventory turns.
  • Pick rate (lines/hr), walk distance, order cycle time, accuracy.
  • First-time-right part rate, return rate, CX CSAT/NPS.
  • Gross margin by channel; aged inventory & write-downs.
