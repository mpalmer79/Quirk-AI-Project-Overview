Dealership Employee-Presence Monitoring — What’s Actually Used

Common Today

Dealers widely use biometric time clocks (fingerprint/face) to stop buddy-punching and geofenced or beacon-gated mobile punches to ensure on-site clock-ins.

Cameras in Dealerships

IP camera systems with analytics (LPR, alerts, remote monitoring) are common for security and operations, but they’re typically kept separate from payroll/attendance.

Why Camera → Timecard Is Rare

  • Legal risk (e.g., biometric privacy laws) and class-action exposure.
  • Culture & trust: continuous visual tracking tied to HR records is sensitive.
  • Governance burden: consent, retention, appeals, and auditing.

Practical Path for Quirk

  • Tier 1: Geofencing + Microfence-style beacons; optional kiosk biometric clocks.
  • Tier 2: Use cameras for security/ops; don’t feed them into payroll systems.

Governance Must-Haves

  • Written notice & consent; clear retention/deletion policy.
  • Security controls; employee appeal path for mismatches.
  • Provide a non-biometric alternative for clock-ins.

Forward-Looking

Expect tighter state rules and vendor offerings that emphasize privacy-by-design (edge processing, minimal retention, and consent workflows) over broad video-to-payroll integrations.
