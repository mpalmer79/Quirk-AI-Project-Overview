Is it possible?

Facial recognition software that integrates with Paycom. Check employee activity while on-premises against their timecard. Are they still on site while clocked in? Let’s design an AI-powered approach.

No current off-the-shelf product continuously checks activity vs. timecards in Paycom using facial recognition, but it’s technically feasible: vendors offer AI-powered time clocks and camera analytics that can integrate with HR platforms.

How a continuous monitoring system could work

This is more invasive than a standard time clock and must be privacy- and law-first.

  • Surveillance hardware: AI cameras beyond entrances, covering key zones.
  • Facial tracking: Match faces to a registered employee gallery in near-real time.
  • Premise verification: Confirm on-site presence while clocked in; alert if absent or out-of-bounds.
  • Activity cross-reference: Compare camera events with Paycom timecards (API) to flag discrepancies.

Legal and ethical concerns

Continuous tracking heightens legal, ethical, and cultural risks.

  • Consent & notice: States like IL/TX/WA regulate biometrics (e.g., BIPA) → written notice & consent.
  • Litigation risk: Class-action exposure over biometric privacy violations.
  • Privacy & morale: Persistent monitoring can erode trust and chill the workplace.
  • Bias: FR systems can misidentify women/people of color → discriminatory outcomes.
  • Mission creep: Drift into productivity scoring without renewed consent.

Paycom-friendly, lower-risk options

Alternatives that discourage time theft without constant facial tracking:

  • Microfence: Bluetooth beacons; app clock-ins only within zone.
  • Geofencing: GPS boundary for clock-in/out via the app.
  • App biometrics: Face/Touch ID to unlock the app (distinct from live surveillance).
